## Problem Statement
The ads in `mammamiradio` are already funny, but they should become one of the main reasons people keep listening. The goal is not "insert a commercial break." The goal is "people hear an ad and think, wait, this station is doing something weirdly great."

You want the ads to feel produced and collectible: ambient noises, jingles, different speakers, recurring bits, and whacky themes that still sound like real radio craft instead of random LLM chaos.

## What Makes This Cool
Most AI radio treats ads as filler. That is leaving the best toy on the floor.

The cool version is that ads become a parallel comedy universe inside the station:
- fake brands with lore
- recurring spokespeople with instantly recognizable delivery
- sonic worlds that change by product category and campaign
- tiny audio details that make the whole station feel "expensive" even when the joke is stupid

If this lands, listeners start quoting ads back to each other. That is the whole game.

## Constraints
- This is an existing Python FastAPI project with the ad pipeline already routed through `mammamiradio/scriptwriter.py`, `mammamiradio/tts.py`, `mammamiradio/normalizer.py`, and `mammamiradio/producer.py`.
- The current ad system already supports structured ad parts, a single ad voice per spot, a small SFX vocabulary, mood-based beds, recurring brands, and campaign history.
- The runtime needs to keep producing segments reliably. Ad quality can go up, but the failure mode still needs to stay graceful.
- Audio generation is local and procedural in places. Anything proposed should work even when there is no giant asset pipeline.
- The station identity is Italian radio. The ads should feel native to that world, not like generic English-language sketch comedy translated into Italian.

## Premises
- The biggest gap is not "more jokes." It is stronger format.
- Distinctiveness comes from repeatable systems, not from asking the model to be more creative.
- "Produced" is mostly about contrast and consistency: a recognizable speaker, a category-specific bed, one or two signature sounds, and a structure the listener can feel.
- Recurring ad campaigns are more memorable than one-off absurdity.
- Ambient texture matters because it implies location and story. A beach club ad should sound like a beach club. A late-night call-in psychic ad should sound like velvet curtains and bad decisions.

## Approaches Considered
### Approach A: Better Copy, Same Production Model
Keep the current rendering pipeline mostly intact and improve prompts, brand definitions, and voice casting. Add more ad archetypes, stronger campaign arcs, and better hooks/taglines, but keep each ad as one main speaker plus light SFX.

Why this is attractive:
- low engineering risk
- fits the current `AdScript` and `AdPart` shape
- easy to ship incrementally

Why it tops out:
- the ads may get funnier, but they still will not feel truly "produced"
- different brands will blur together because the sound design grammar is too narrow
- the station misses the chance to make ads audibly premium

### Approach B: Signature Ad System
Turn ads into a real sub-format with explicit creative templates, sonic palettes, speaker roles, and campaign memory. Each ad break still uses the existing pipeline, but the system now decides:
- what kind of ad this is
- what sonic environment it lives in
- whether it uses a solo pitch, duo dialogue, announcer plus disclaimer, caller testimonial, or fake live remote
- what recurring motif belongs to this brand

Why this is stronger:
- it creates recognizable texture, not just better text
- it makes "different speakers" and "whacky themes" first-class instead of occasional luck
- it gives the LLM narrower rails, which usually produces better output, not worse

Cost:
- more schema design
- more prompt design
- a slightly richer audio assembly layer

## Recommended Approach
Choose Approach B.

Not because it is bigger, because it is the first version that actually matches the ambition. You do not want "slightly funnier ads." You want ads that feel like a hidden feature people discover and then start waiting for.

The right product move is to define an ad creative system with four layers.

### 1. Ad Formats
Each generated ad should declare a format before writing copy. Start with 6:
- `classic_pitch`: one aggressive announcer, fast close, clean joke
- `testimonial`: customer voice plus announcer button
- `duo_scene`: two characters arguing, one of them clearly losing
- `live_remote`: field reporter at a ridiculous event or business
- `late_night_whisper`: intimate, sensual, slightly cursed
- `institutional_psa`: serious public-service tone for something completely deranged

This matters because listeners can feel the shape of the joke faster than the words.

### 2. Sonic Worlds
Each ad should also declare a sonic palette. Not just a generic "mood." A more useful set:
- environment bed: cafe, motorway, beach, showroom, stadium, luxury spa, occult basement, shopping channel
- music bed: lounge, tarantella-pop, cheap synth romance, overblown epic, suspicious jazz, discount techno
- transition motif: chime, tape stop, register hit, whoosh, hotline beep, mandolin sting

This is where ambient noises become a real feature instead of an afterthought.

### 3. Speaker Casting
Different speakers should be treated as roles, not just random voices. Build a cast like a radio comedy troupe:
- `The Hammer`: booming national TV voice
- `The Seductress`: whisper-ASMR menace
- `The Bureaucrat`: dry official notice voice
- `The Maniac`: oversold shopping-channel operator
- `The Witness`: fake customer testimonial
- `The Disclaimer Goblin`: ultra-fast legal cleanup

Some ads should use two voices. That is a big unlock. The fast disclaimer reader becomes much funnier when the main pitch is self-important and polished.

### 4. Campaign Memory
Every recurring brand should have a campaign spine:
- core premise
- recurring sonic signature
- preferred format pool
- recurring spokesperson
- escalation rule

Example:
- `Negroni as a Service`
- signature: ice clink plus startup synth bloom
- format pool: `classic_pitch`, `institutional_psa`, `live_remote`
- spokesperson: The Hammer
- escalation: every new spot reveals a worse subscription tier

This turns ad history from "previous summary text" into actual serial comedy.

## Open Questions
- How far should the station go on multi-voice ads before generation cost and assembly complexity become annoying?
- Should brands stay fully fictional, or can there be parody versions of familiar Italian product categories without creating legal weirdness?
- Do you want some ads to recur almost every show like radio mascots, or keep recurrence lighter so breaks stay surprising?
- Should jingles be brand-owned motifs, break-owned motifs, or both?

## Success Criteria
- Listeners can identify at least 3 ad brands or speaker personas by sound alone.
- Two ads for the same brand feel related, not merely repeated.
- Breaks have audible contrast between categories and themes.
- The ads still work when the model has a mediocre generation day because the format carries part of the quality.
- The station feels more premium and more authored after the change, not just more noisy.

## Distribution Plan
This ships as part of the existing `mammamiradio` app.

No new distribution surface is needed. The current FastAPI app, Docker path, and Home Assistant add-on already carry the feature. The existing deployment pipeline covers this.

The real distribution win is social, not technical. Once the ads are strong enough, they become clip-worthy moments for demos, screenshots, screen recordings, and "listen to what this fake station just played" shares.

## Next Steps
1. Expand the ad data model so a brand can define campaign spine, preferred formats, sonic palette, and recurring spokesperson.
2. Expand `AdScript` so generation can request multiple speaker roles, richer SFX/environment cues, and a reusable jingle tag.
3. Rewrite the ad prompt in `mammamiradio/scriptwriter.py` around explicit ad format selection and sonic-world selection instead of one generic prompt.
4. Extend audio assembly in `mammamiradio/tts.py` and `mammamiradio/normalizer.py` so ads can support layered ambience, per-role voices, and short brand motifs without becoming fragile.
5. Add fixture-heavy tests around campaign continuity, format variety, and fallback behavior in `tests/test_ads.py`, `tests/test_tts.py`, and `tests/test_normalizer_extended.py`.
6. Curate a first "starter pack" of 8-10 flagship brands that each prove a different ad format. These are the heroes that teach the system how to sound.
